A home care consultation should make the next decision clearer. It is not a test, and your family does not need to arrive with a completed care plan.

Blue River Home Care offers a free discussion by phone or in person with the client, family, or both.

## Start with what is happening now

The most useful starting point is an ordinary day. Explain which routines are working, who already helps, and which parts of the day create concern or strain.

It also helps to share the client's preferences. A plan is more likely to work when it respects how the person wants to live and how comfortable they feel with outside help.

## Decide who should join the conversation

Home care decisions often involve a spouse, adult children, relatives, or friends. The consultation can clarify who is making decisions, who currently provides help, and where responsibilities have become difficult to sustain.

You can include the people who should hear the same information. There is no need to make one family member carry the entire conversation alone.

## Discuss the care plan and schedule

If private-pay caregiving appears to fit, Blue River can explain how the client and family build the care plan together. The proposed schedule comes from that plan and the client's preferences.

The conversation can also cover private-pay expectations and questions about an existing insurance policy, Veterans or survivor benefits, Masonic assistance, or another subsidy program. The insurer or program decides eligibility and coverage.

## Leave with a next step

The next step may be another conversation, more information from the family, confirmation of service availability, or movement toward a care plan. A good consultation can still be useful even if Blue River is not ultimately the right fit.
