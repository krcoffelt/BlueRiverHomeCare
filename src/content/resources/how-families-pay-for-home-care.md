Blue River Home Care is focused on private-pay care. That means the client or family is responsible for payment unless an insurer or assistance program confirms that a benefit applies.

Many families still pay at least part of the cost out of pocket. The useful question is not whether a funding source exists in general, but whether it applies to this person, this policy, and this care plan.

## Start with care, then price the plan

Before depending on outside funding, ask Chris what services and schedule the family is considering. A realistic care plan makes it easier to estimate the private-pay responsibility and explain the proposed care to an insurer or assistance program.

Do not delay the first conversation while trying to solve every payment question. Keep care planning and benefit verification as two connected but separate steps.

## Insurance coverage varies by policy

Some long-term care or other private insurance policies may include benefits for care at home. Coverage, waiting periods, documentation, provider requirements, and reimbursement rules vary by policy.

The [Missouri Department of Commerce and Insurance](https://insurance.mo.gov/long-term-care-insurance) recommends reviewing what a long-term care policy actually covers. Contact the insurer directly and ask whether the proposed in-home care and provider qualify under the policy.

## Ask about Veterans and survivor benefits

Qualified Veterans and survivors may be eligible for VA pension additions such as Aid and Attendance or Housebound benefits. The VA explains that these are monthly payments added to a qualifying pension, not automatic coverage for every home care client.

Review the current [VA Aid and Attendance requirements](https://www.va.gov/pension/aid-attendance-housebound/) or [survivor benefit information](https://www.va.gov/family-and-caregiver-benefits/survivor-compensation/) with the VA or an accredited representative. Blue River cannot determine eligibility or approve a claim.

## The Masonic Home may offer assistance

The Masonic Home of Missouri offers financial assistance programs for eligible Missouri Masons, wives, widows, and Eastern Star members who demonstrate financial need. Applications are evaluated by the program on a case-by-case basis.

Families with a qualifying Masonic connection can review the [Masonic Home of Missouri financial programs](https://www.mohome.org/financial-programs/) and contact that organization directly about eligibility and allowable expenses.

## Each outside benefit requires program approval

Chris can help your family organize what to ask about an insurance policy, Veterans or surviving-spouse benefits, Masonic assistance, or another subsidy program. Blue River does not guarantee eligibility, reimbursement, approval, or payment from any outside source.

Do not begin care assuming a third party will pay until that organization confirms the benefit in writing.
